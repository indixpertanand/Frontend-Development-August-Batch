Politics in India

Introduction

India, the world’s largest democracy, has a vibrant and diverse political landscape. Governed by a federal parliamentary system, India has multiple political parties representing a wide range of ideologies and interests. The country’s political framework is guided by the Constitution of India, which was adopted in 1950.

Historical Background

Pre-Independence Era

Before independence in 1947, Indian politics was dominated by the Indian National Congress (INC), which led the struggle against British rule. Other political groups and movements, such as the Muslim League and various socialist and communist factions, also played significant roles.

Structure of Indian Politics

The Three Branches of Government

Executive – Led by the President, Prime Minister, and Council of Ministers, responsible for policy implementation.

Legislature – Consists of two houses, the Lok Sabha (House of the People) and the Rajya Sabha (Council of States), responsible for making laws.

Judiciary – An independent body, headed by the Supreme Court, ensuring the rule of law and constitutional compliance.
